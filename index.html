Shardhana Project

Open-source structural simulation: from Classical Hand Calculations toward HEM (Han Element Method)

The Beginning of the Project

Shardhana began with the goal of implementing the Applied Element Method (AEM) as an open-source project.

While the traditional Finite Element Method (FEM) has certain limitations in naturally representing post-failure behavior, AEM drew attention for its ability to simulate more intuitive collapse behavior through element separation and re-contact.

The project was initiated with the intention of directly implementing the core principles of structural analysis and sharing them openly, without relying on proprietary software.

Expansion of Direction

In striving to overcome the limitations of FEM, we came to recognize that AEM may also entail fundamental limitations.

  • What is an element?
  • Is failure a discontinuity, or a transformation of state?
  • Is structural behavior the result of matrix operations, or a continuous process of change?

These questions became the catalyst for expanding the direction of the project.

Transition Toward HEM

Shardhana began by pursuing AEM beyond FEM, and along the way, it evolved toward a new conceptual analysis core: HEM.

HEM interprets an element not merely as a stiffness block, but as a seed-like state unit composed of a Core and a Face.

Core represents the intrinsic material and energy state, while Face represents the boundary through which interaction occurs.

Failure is no longer interpreted as simple â€œbreakage,â€ but as the reconfiguration and differentiation of states. Collapse is not discontinuity, but part of transformation.

Goals of Shardhana

Shardhana aims to:

  • Implement an open-source structural analysis core
  • Organize and interpret fundamental FEM theory
  • Explore AEM analytical concepts
  • Establish HEM as a new analytical methodology
  • Develop simulations that approach natural phenomena more closely

Nature is the teacher of Shardhana.
